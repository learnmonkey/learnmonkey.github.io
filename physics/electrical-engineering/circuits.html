Circuits

What are Circuits?

A circuit is a loop that electrons can travel through. If we don't make it a loop, the electrons can't travel. Circuits include a source of electricity, like a battery. Circuits also generally include a load, which is something that uses up electricity, like a light bulb.

The components of circuits are called electric components.

We can also have a switch. A switch blocks the flow of the electrons. If the electrons are blocked, we call the circuit an open circuit. If the electrons are flowing, we call the circuit a closed circuit.
