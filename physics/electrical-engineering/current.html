Current

In electrical engineering, current is the rate of electricity (usually electrons) that is flowing past a point in a circuit.

If you think of the electricity as water flow, current is how much water is flowing through a point in the pipe every second.

The SI unit for measuring current is the ampere (symbol: A). An ampere is defined as one coulomb per second. We measure current using an ammeter.
