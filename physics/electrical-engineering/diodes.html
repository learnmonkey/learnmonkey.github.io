Diodes

What is a Diode?

Diodes are electrical components that block the flow of electricity in one direction, but allow it in the opposite direction. The direction in which the diode blocks electricity is called the reverse direction, while the opposite direction is called the forward direction.

Uses

Diodes have a wide variety of uses.

How Diodes Work

Diodes are made of semiconductors. In particular, the are made up of a p-type doped semiconductor stuck onto an n-type doped semiconductor.

If you run the electricity in the forward direction, the electrons in the n-type side move to fill the holes in the p-type side and the electricity flows.

However, if you have the electricity go in the reverse direction, the electrons and the electron holes are drawn to either side and no electricity will flow.

Light Emitting Diodes (LEDs)

Light emitting diodes are a type of diode that emit light when current is run through it.
