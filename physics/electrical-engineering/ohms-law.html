Ohm's Law

What is Ohm's Law?

Ohm's law relates voltage, current, and resistance in a nice, simple equation:

Voltage = Current x Resistance

Voltage is usually abbreviated as V, current as I, and resistance as R. So, you will usually see Ohm's law written as:

V = IR

Ohm's law is named after German physicist Georg Ohm, who published this equation in 1827.

Using Ohm's Law

If we know current and resistance, we can calculate the voltage using Ohm's law:

V = IR

If we know voltage and resistance, we can calculate the current by dividing both sides of Ohm's law by the resistance:

I = V/R

If we know voltage and current, we can calculate resistance by dividing both sides of Ohm's law by current:

R = V/I

Resistance can't be measured while a circuit is operating, so if we need the resistance, we can simply use Ohm's law.
