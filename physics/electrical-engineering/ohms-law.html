Ohm's Law

What is Ohm's Law?

Ohm's law relates voltage, current, and resistance in a nice, simple equation:

Voltage = Current x Resistance

Voltage is usually abbreviated as V, current as I, and resistance as R. So, you will usually see Ohm's law written as:

V = IR

Ohm's law is named after German physicist Georg Ohm, who published this equation in 1827.
