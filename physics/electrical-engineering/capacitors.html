Capacitors

What are Capacitors?

Capacitors store electricity, just like batteries. The difference is that capacitors charge and release electricity much faster. This is because capacitors store electricity using electric fields, while batteries use chemistry.

Uses

Capacitors have a wide variety of applications in many different fields.

Capacitance

The effect of a capacitor is called capacitance. We measure capacitance in farads (symbol: F).

How Capacitors Work

Capacitors consists of two metal plates, called the electrodes, with an insulator in the middle called the dielectric. Because the electrons cannot cross the dielectric, there is a buildup of charge, which can be harnessed for electricity.
