Electrical Power

What is Electrical Power?

Electrical power is the rate of electrical energy that something uses or produces. Electrical power can be calculated using the following formula:

Power = Current x Voltage

You will most often see the formula as:

P = IV

Using the Electrical Power Equation

The electrical power equation is quite useful because the electrical power output of something or the electrical power used by something remains the same when we change the voltage or current.

Electricity transmission, in fact, uses high voltage because of electrical power. First of all, let's remind ourselves of the equation for power:

P = IV

Here, we can use Ohm's Law:

V = IR

We can substitute the voltage in the power equation for Ohm's law, so now we have:

P = I2R

We can then substiute in the current using Ohm's law:

P = (V / R)2 x R

Simplifying, we get:

P = V2 / R

Power is constant because the power plant can only generate so much power. So, if we increase voltage, the resistance must go down. So, the power plants will increase the voltage a lot, so that the resistance will be lower and less electricity is wasted.
