Conductors and Insulators

Inside the Atom

Atoms are made up of two parts: the electrons and the nucleus. The nucleus is an tiny and incredibly compact object in the center of the atom. The nucleus is made up of protons and neutrons. A proton has the charge of an electron, but positive. A neutron has a charge of zero. The electrons are in the outer region of the atom.

What is a Conductor?

A conductor is a material that electricity can pass through. Most metals are conductive. We use conductors to make wires. Some materials are more conductive than others.

What is an Insulator?

An insulator is a material that electricity can't pass through. This includes rubber and ceramic. We use insulators to cover the conductor when making wires so that people aren't injured by the electricity. Electricity can be dangerous!
