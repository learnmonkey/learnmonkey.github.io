Conductors and Insulators

Inside the Atom

Atoms are made up of two parts: the electrons and the nucleus. The nucleus is an tiny and incredibly compact object in the center of the atom. The nucleus is made up of protons and neutrons. A proton has the charge of an electron, but positive. A neutron has a charge of zero. The electrons are in the outer region of the atom.

Conductors

A conductor is a material that electricity can pass through. Most metals are conductive. We use conductors to make wires. Some materials are more conductive than others.

Insulators

An insulator is a material that electricity can't pass through. This includes rubber and ceramic. We use insulators to cover the conductor when making wires so that people aren't injured by the electricity. Electricity can be dangerous!

Semiconductors

In addition to conductors and insulators, there is also the less common semiconductor. Semiconductors conduct electricity better than insulators, but worse than conductors. Semiconductors are used in diodes and transistors.

What Makes Something Conductive?

You can think of electrons orbiting around the nucleus in different circles, called orbitals, depending on how much energy they have. There are a finite number of orbitals.The last orbital that electrons are in is called the valence band.

There is also another band called the conduction band. If an electron reaches this band, the electron can escape the atom and go to other atoms.

In conductors, the valence band and the conduction band overlap, so it is very easy for electrons to escape the atom. However, in insulators, the atom has a tight grip on the electrons and the conduction band is far away, so the electrons cannot escape.

In semiconductors, the valence band and the conduction band are close together, but they do not overlap.
